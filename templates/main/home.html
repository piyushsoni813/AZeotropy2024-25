About AZeotropy

AZeotropy, India's largest Chemical Engineering Symposium, is a unique blend of intriguing competitions, lecture series, panel discussions, exhibitions, workshops, and other learning-related activities. As a non-profit organization, we aim to bridge the gap between students, industry, and academia.

Our mission is to manifest the spirit of Chemical Engineering in young students from all corners of India. We encourage participation from over 350 colleges, 50+ industrialists, and welcome an average footfall of 12,000 attendees.

The symposium has hosted lectures by eminent personalities, including Gerald Fuller, Professor of Chemical Engineering at Stanford University, Douglas Buttrey, Professor at the Department of Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering, University of Delaware, Abraham D. Stroock, Cornell University, and Harsha Bhogle, Indian cricket commentator and journalist and more.

Sangtae (Sang) Kim is a Distinguished Professor at Purdue University, recognized for his pioneering work in microfluidics and fluidic self-assembly. His research has revolutionized the production of RFID tags and other microelectronic devices. Prior to joining Purdue, he held leadership roles in industry and government, including at the National Science Foundation. Dr. Kim is a member of the National Academy of Engineering.
